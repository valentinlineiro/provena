# Philosophy

Professional identity should belong to the professional, not to any platform.

Platforms are transient. Knowledge is durable.

Provena models professional identity as structured knowledge and treats LinkedIn, résumés, personal websites, and conference bios as projections of the same canonical source.

## The problem

The same information is manually duplicated across platforms.

LinkedIn, résumé, GitHub profile, speaker bio, conference proposal.

These inevitably diverge.

Maintenance becomes overhead. Inconsistencies erode credibility.

## The solution

A single source of truth. Deterministic projections.

```
Edit once.
Represent everywhere.
```

## The principle

Knowledge, transformation, and representation are separate concerns.

- **Knowledge** — the canonical model of who you are and what you have done.
- **Transformation** — selecting and reshaping that knowledge for a specific context.
- **Representation** — writing the result for a specific medium.

No platform owns your identity. LinkedIn, recruiters, and conference committees consume projections. They do not define the source.

## The commitment

Provena will never:

- Lock your identity into a proprietary format.
- Sell your data.
- Require a platform account.
- Make you choose between convenience and ownership.

## The trade-off

A canonical model requires discipline. You maintain one source instead of updating multiple copies. When the discipline slips, the model diverges — but a single source diverges less than five independent copies.

The alternative is the current state: fragmentation by default.

## The long view

Platforms evolve. Your professional history should remain under your control.

The best way to guarantee that is to own it yourself, in a format you control, with tools you can run offline.

Own your identity. Project it anywhere.
